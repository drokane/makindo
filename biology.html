üß¨ Cell Biology

  • Cells: Know differences between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells, and the function of organelles like nucleus, mitochondria, and ribosomes.
  • Microscopy: Use light and electron microscopes to compare resolution and magnification. Know how to prepare slides and calculate image size.
  • Cell Division: Mitosis produces identical cells for growth and repair. Stem cells can differentiate and are used in medicine.
  • Transport: Diffusion, osmosis, and active transport are key methods by which substances move in and out of cells.

üß´ Organisation

  • Cell Organisation: Cells ‚Üí Tissues ‚Üí Organs ‚Üí Organ systems. Each level works together to maintain function.
  • Digestive System: Enzymes (amylase, protease, lipase) break down food. Bile emulsifies fat and neutralises acid.
  • Circulatory System: Double circulatory system transports oxygen and nutrients. Includes heart, blood vessels, and blood components.
  • Disease: Communicable and non-communicable diseases; risk factors include lifestyle, environment, and genetics.
  • Cancer: Caused by uncontrolled cell growth. Know the difference between benign and malignant tumours.

  • Respiration: Releases energy. Aerobic uses oxygen; anaerobic does not and produces less energy. In humans: glucose ‚Üí lactic acid.
  • Metabolism: All the chemical reactions in the body. Includes respiration, protein synthesis, and breaking down toxins.

ü¶† Infection and Response

  • Pathogens: Bacteria, viruses, fungi, and protists cause disease. Spread by air, water, direct contact.
  • Body Defences: Skin, mucus, cilia, white blood cells (phagocytosis, antibodies, antitoxins).
  • Vaccination: Dead/inactive pathogens stimulate immune memory. Prevents future infection.
  • Drugs: Painkillers relieve symptoms; antibiotics kill bacteria. Resistance occurs if antibiotics are overused.
  • Drug Development: Preclinical and clinical trials test safety and effectiveness of new medicines.

üß¨ Genetics and Inheritance

  • DNA: Carries genetic code. Made of bases A, T, C, G. Genes code for proteins.
  • Inheritance: Dominant and recessive alleles, Punnett squares, genotype vs phenotype.
  • Inherited Disorders: Cystic fibrosis (recessive), polydactyly (dominant). Can be screened in embryos.
  • Variation: Caused by genes, environment, or both. Continuous vs discontinuous.
  • Selective Breeding and Genetic Engineering: Used to improve organisms. Genetic modification involves inserting genes from one species into another.

üêí Evolution and Ecology

  • Natural Selection: Darwin‚Äôs theory ‚Äî survival of the fittest. Evidence includes fossils and antibiotic resistance.
  • Extinction: Caused by rapid environmental change, new predators, competition, disease.
  • Classification: Organising organisms into groups. Carl Linnaeus and the 3-domain system.
  • Ecosystems: Interdependence, food chains, food webs. Producers, consumers, decomposers.
  • Carbon and Water Cycles: Essential for nutrient recycling and maintaining life on Earth.
  • Biodiversity: Variety of life in an area. Maintained by conservation, sustainable farming, breeding programs.
