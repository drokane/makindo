GCSE: Chemistry

⚗️ Chemistry Top Tips

  • Learn Common Ions and Symbols: You'll need these for writing chemical equations.
  • Balance Equations Frequently: This is a must-have skill for exams.
  • Revise Bonding Types: Understand the structure and properties of ionic, covalent and metallic bonding.
  • Practise with Required Practicals: Know the methods and what each step is testing.
  • Work on Moles and Calculations: Show your steps clearly — avoid skipping logic.
  • Understand Periodic Trends: Know what happens to reactivity across and down the table.
  • Use Real Exam Questions: Chemistry mark schemes are very specific — practise phrasing.

⚗️ GCSE Chemistry

  • Atomic Structure: Understand protons, neutrons, electrons, atomic and mass numbers, and electronic configurations.
  • Periodic Table Trends: Know why elements are arranged by atomic number and grouped by reactivity — especially groups 1, 7, and 0.
  • Bonding & Structure: Be able to compare ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding. Draw dot-and-cross diagrams accurately.
  • Chemical Equations: Practise balancing equations and identifying reactants, products, and state symbols.
  • Quantitative Chemistry: Master mole calculations, mass, concentration, and percentage yield questions.
  • Acids and Bases: Learn the pH scale, reactions of acids with bases and metals, and the importance of titration.
  • Electrolysis: Understand how and why electrolysis separates ionic substances — and apply it to practical and industrial processes.
  • Energy Changes: Distinguish between exothermic and endothermic reactions, and interpret energy profile diagrams.
  • Rates of Reaction: Learn collision theory and how temperature, concentration, pressure, and catalysts affect rate.
  • Earth and Atmospheric Science: Know how the Earth's atmosphere evolved, the carbon cycle, and the impact of human activity.
